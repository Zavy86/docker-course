# Contribute

A huge thank you for dedicating your time and energy to following my entire Docker course!

I sincerely hope this material has been extremely useful to you and that the knowledge you have gained will help you 
elevate your skills and projects. Your growth is the best reward for the work I have done.

If you would like to give back, there are three important ways you can contribute to my online educational project...


## Share this course

If you found this course valuable, the simplest and most effective way to support me is to share it!

Help your friends, colleagues, and community discover this course and encourage them to follow it.

You can share the link to the GitHub repository:
[https://github.com/zavy86/docker-course](https://github.com/zavy86/docker-course)

Or share the complete playlist on YouTube:
[https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLdYHjrPuGeKEhYCXvSJsqf5zsECH-JXFp](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLdYHjrPuGeKEhYCXvSJsqf5zsECH-JXFp)


## Contribute to the project

This is an open-source project, and I would love for it to thrive and grow thanks to the community!

If you spot an error in the text, slides, or source code, please open an
[issue](https://github.com/Zavy86/docker-course/issues).

If you are interested in translating the course into another language (an amazing contribution!) or have suggestions to
improve the organization or content, please open a
[pull request](https://github.com/Zavy86/docker-course/pulls).

Every contribution is highly appreciated!


## Make a Donation

Creating comprehensive and free courses requires significant time and resources.

If you would like to directly support my content creation activities (new courses, updates, and maintenance), you can 
make a small donation via
[GitHub](https://github.com/sponsors/Zavy86)
or, if you prefer, also via
[PayPal](https://www.paypal.com/paypalme/zavy86).

Every contribution, even the smallest, motivates me to continue producing high-quality and accessible material for 
everyone.
